10 Places To Visit In Batticaloa For A True Sri Lankan Experience

Batticaloa is a town situated in the Eastern region of Sri Lanka. It is located on the east coast, 69 miles south by south-east of Trincomalee, and is arranged on an island. Rich in distinct culture, traditions, and lush nature, Batticaloa has plenty to offer to people planning a visit! It is also the perfect place to grasp the Tamil lifestyle. The slow-paced town makes it a great place to refresh oneself from the stress of everyday life!

Most Spectacular Places of Batticaloa

BATTICALOA FORT
Batticaloa Fort, also known as Dutch Fort, is surrounded by lush greenery and lagoons. Originally constructed by the Portuguese in 1628, it boasts impressive thick walls and an encompassing scenic view of the serene lagoon. Itâ€™s a must-visit for history enthusiasts!

KALLADY BEACH
Kallady Beach offers a stunning shoreline on Sri Lanka's east coast. This tranquil spot is ideal for an evening stroll, allowing you to admire the sunset and relax in a peaceful environment.

KALLADY BRIDGE
The Kallady Bridge is famous for its "Singing Fish" phenomenon, where faint melodic sounds rise from the waters during certain months. Visitors can experience this unique natural occurrence using oars to amplify the sounds.

BATTICALOA LIGHTHOUSE
Although not accessible to visitors, this lighthouse provides an iconic sight in Batticaloa. A boat trip around the lagoon offers breathtaking views of the lighthouse and surrounding areas.
